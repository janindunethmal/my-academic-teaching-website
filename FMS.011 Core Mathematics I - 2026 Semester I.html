FMS.011 - Core Mathematics I (2026 Semester I - GISM)

Credits: 15  |  Lectures: 12 Sessions × 3 Hours = 36 Hours  |  Tutorials: 12 Sessions × 1 Hour = 12 Hours

Module Overview

This module provides a rigorous foundation in mathematical reasoning. Students will learn the language of mathematics through algebra, systems of linear equations, matrices, vectors, calculus fundamentals, mathematical modelling, and their applications. The course is designed to build the mathematical maturity required for further studies in mathematics, engineering, and the sciences.

Intended Learning Outcomes

  • Describe and apply methods for solving systems of linear equations using algebraic and matrix-based techniques.
  • Demonstrate knowledge and understanding of matrices and their operations.
  • Apply vectors, lines, and planes in two- and three-dimensional space.
  • Demonstrate an understanding of limits and continuity of functions.
  • Calculate derivatives and integrals of simple functions and apply them to practical applications.
  • Translate real-world problems into mathematical models.
  • Demonstrate computational skills using mathematical software tools.
