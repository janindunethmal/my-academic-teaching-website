FMS.021 - Core Mathematics II (2026 Semester II - GISM)

Credits: 15  |  Lectures: 12 Sessions × 3 Hours = 36 Hours  |  Tutorials: 12 Sessions × 1 Hour = 12 Hours

Module Overview

This module builds on Core Mathematics I and covers complex numbers, eigenvalues and eigenvectors, linear programming, first-order differential equations, integration techniques, mathematical modelling, and computational methods. Students will develop a deep understanding of advanced mathematical concepts with applications in engineering and the physical sciences.

Intended Learning Outcomes

  • Demonstrate proficiency with the algebra and geometry of complex numbers.
  • Translate problems into mathematical models and apply the concepts and techniques of basic calculus and linear algebra to a wide variety of applications.
  • Use computer software (such as MATLAB) to carry out graphical, numerical and symbolic investigations.
  • Calculate determinants, eigenvalues and eigenvectors of up to 3-by-3 matrices.
  • Formulate, solve and analyse linear programming problems.
  • Solve simple first-order differential equations and competently calculate integrals.
