Course Overview

The Intensive Mathematics Course is designed to provide incoming undergraduate students of the Faculty of Technology with the mathematical foundations required for university-level studies.

The course emphasizes fundamental mathematical concepts and problem-solving techniques essential for engineering, technology and scientific applications.

Through lectures, worked examples, tutorials and continuous assessments, students will develop analytical thinking and mathematical confidence before beginning their undergraduate studies.

Course Objectives

Students completing this course should be able to:

  1. Explain the importance of mathematics in engineering and technology.
  2. Perform calculations involving fractions, decimals, percentages, ratios and proportions.
  3. Simplify algebraic expressions.
  4. Apply algebraic identities.
  5. Evaluate engineering formulae.
  6. Solve linear equations.
  7. Solve simultaneous equations.
  8. Solve quadratic equations.
  9. Solve inequalities.
  10. Apply logarithmic laws.
  11. Solve logarithmic equations.
  12. Apply factorization techniques.
  13. Perform partial fraction decomposition.
  14. Understand matrices and perform basic matrix operations.
  15. Understand functions and graphs.
  16. Understand trigonometry and basic identities.
  17. Understand introductory differentiation.
  18. Apply derivatives to engineering problems.
  19. Understand introductory integration.
  20. Develop analytical and mathematical problem-solving skills.

Independent Learning

Self-Study Expectations

Students are expected to revise lecture material daily to reinforce their understanding of the concepts covered. Regular review ensures that knowledge is consolidated and any gaps in understanding are identified early.

Completion of all tutorial exercises is essential for developing problem-solving skills. Students should attempt each tutorial independently before seeking assistance, using lecture notes and reference materials as guides.

Independent quiz preparation is strongly encouraged. Students should attempt sample problems and review past exercises to build confidence and readiness for the formal assessments.

Preparation for the final examination through structured self-study is critical. Students should allocate sufficient time for comprehensive revision of all 10 days of course content, focusing on both theoretical understanding and practical application.
