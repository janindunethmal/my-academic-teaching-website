MAT 1100 - Mathematical Software (USJ)

Department: Department of Mathematics  |  Institution: University of Sri Jayewardenepura (USJ)

Module Overview

MAT 1100 Mathematical Software is an introductory course offered by the Department of Mathematics at the University of Sri Jayewardenepura. The course introduces students to the use of mathematical software tools for exploring and solving problems across a range of mathematical topics including number theory, linear algebra, calculus and vector calculus.

The primary software focus of this course is Maple, a powerful computer algebra system used extensively in mathematical computation, symbolic manipulation, and visualisation.

Through hands-on practical sessions, students will develop computational skills and learn how to apply software-based methods to visualise, analyse and solve mathematical problems encountered in their degree programme.

In 2026 Second Semester, this course was conducted under the role of Lecturer in Charge, with responsibilities including:

  • Delivering lectures on essential mathematical software tools and computational techniques, with a primary focus on Maple.
  • Preparing, evaluating, and discussing assignments, exercises, and the end-semester examination.
  • Oversight of tutorial sessions, including instructing and guiding students to enhance their understanding and overall learning experience.

Intended Learning Outcomes

  • Demonstrate proficiency in using mathematical software for computational tasks.
  • Explore concepts in number theory using computational tools.
  • Write basic programs to solve mathematical problems.
  • Perform matrix operations and solve systems of linear equations using software.
  • Apply numerical and symbolic integration techniques using computational tools.
  • Visualise and analyse vector spaces and vector calculus concepts.
